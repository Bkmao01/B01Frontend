Charlotte ranks as N.C.'s No. 1 degree producer for minority students for 2024

UNC Charlotte has been recognized as the top university in North Carolina and among the top 75 in the nation for awarding bachelor’s degrees to minority students, according to Diverse: Issues in Higher Education’s 2024 Diverse 100 rankings. The university ranks 31st nationally for bachelor’s degrees awarded to African American students and third among North Carolina universities. Additionally, Charlotte is highly ranked for degrees awarded to Asian American and Hispanic/Latinx students.

For African American students, Charlotte ranks in the top 100 for 26 undergraduate programs, with 14 programs in the top 20, including Finance (No. 3), Mathematics (No. 5), and Computer Science (No. 11). For Asian American students, the university has 19 top 100 program rankings, including Engineering Technology (No. 19) and Computer Science (No. 43). Charlotte also leads North Carolina in awarding bachelor’s degrees to Hispanic/Latinx students, with 14 top 100 program rankings, including Finance (No. 19) and Computer Science (No. 38).

Charlotte’s graduate programs are also highly ranked, with eight programs in the top 10. These rankings highlight UNC Charlotte’s commitment to educational success for students of all backgrounds and its status as one of the fastest-rising universities in the nation.

Technical Aspects of the Project: This project is built using Angular for the frontend and Node.js with Express for the backend. The backend communicates with a MySQL database to manage user authentication and data storage. JWT (JSON Web Token) is used for secure authentication. The application is designed as a Single Page Application (SPA) to provide a seamless user experience. The frontend and backend are fully decoupled and communicate through HTTP calls. The project is hosted on a server with NGINX serving the frontend and the backend running on a separate port.
